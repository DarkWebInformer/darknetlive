PSA: White House Market is Retiring

~3 min read | Published on 2021-10-02, tagged Darkweb-Market, Retired using 660 words.
White House Market is retiring, according to a signed message from the administrator.
White House Market announced their retirement on Dread (onion link).
We have reached our goal and now, according to plan, it’s<br>time to for us to retire.

Effective immediately user registration and ordering have<br>been disabled, everything else (yes, withdrawals included)<br>is working as usual.

We will keep the market online for a limited time until<br>open orders are finalized, disputes handled and coins<br>withdrawn. We don’t know how long that will take but<br>don’t expect to come back 6 months from now and find the<br>market up.
All market rules are still in effect so users (both<br>buyers and vendors) should not try to take advantage<br>of the situation and scam, the feedback system is still<br>working and will be shared with Recon and other markets.
Because this week’s featured listings and stickies won’t<br>generate any revenue vendors who paid for them will be<br>refunded via their market wallet.
Vendors can now advertise the markets where they operate<br>on their vendor profiles however direct contacts are still<br>disallowed.
For alternative markets check out /d/Superlist on Dread, the<br>ones listed there are established and going strong. Keep in mind<br>any market can disappear at any time and for what ever<br>reason (exit scam, law enforcement operation, hack, technical<br>issue and so on) but markets like Monopoly (true wallet-less,<br>direct deal) and Versus (enforced multisig) greatly minimize<br>damage. You can also give a chance to new markets, we all<br>have to start somewhere.
Thanks everybody for your business, trust, support and of<br>course for placing decent amounts of money in our pockets.<br>We may come back some time in the future with a different<br>project or we may not. Meanwhile be on the lookout for phishing<br>or copycats, if it’s not signed by us it’s not us.
Good luck and stay safe.

This is, of course, the optimal end for a marketplace. It seems as if their introductory announcement on Dread was not that long ago. White House Market will join the shortlist of markets that retired responsibly.
The market, as of this article, had 894,401 user accounts. Of those, 326,570 are active. Based on the responses to the Dread post and the general sentiment of well-known forum members, White House Market will be missed. And the market’s administrator, Mr. White, will certainly have a lasting impact on the community.
